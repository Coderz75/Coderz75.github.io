# Cambridge 2026
I’m excited to share that my team placed 2nd worldwide at the Grand Finals of Cambridge Battlecode!

This spring, the University of Cambridge hosted its own Battlecode competition sponsored by Jump Trading, Anthropic, and others, as a spin-off of MIT's Battlecode, which I participated in earlier this year. Over six weeks, teams worldwide designed AI agents for a mindustry-style strategy game involving resource management, pathfinding, combat, and other logic. 

After qualifying as one of the top 16 teams worldwide, we advanced to the live double-elimination Grand Finals and finished in 2nd place overall after a series of extremely close matches. The attached clip is one of our games against the eventual 3rd place team, with us playing as gold.

Huge thanks to my awesome teammates Kenny Chau and Matthew Huang. It was a huge learning experience to compete with you guys and I hope to do so again in the future!

Even though I couldn't attend the Grand Finals live at Cambridge, huge thanks to the organizers James Leung, Oliver Fogelin, and the entire Cambridge Battlecode team. This was extremely polished for a first-year competition, and I can only imagine the work all of you put into it! I am looking forward to seeing how it grows in the future!

I hope to have some time in the future to write a postmortem and talk about my experiences!
